The Great Landscapes of the USA

Whenever someone asks me where my favorite place in the world is, this image instantly comes to mind!


Photo Credit: Robert Holloway ® 2018


Above is a picture I took at Glacier Point in Yosemite National Park, CA, located about 4 hours from San Fransisco, California.
Many pair California with palm-lined beaches and "rip your hair out traffic", but few make the effort to leave the bustling cities and venture out into the wilderness.
I have visited Yosemite multiple times, and no other sight compares to the white granite cliffs gleaming in the California sun!

Photo Credit: Robert Holloway ® 2018. Location: Horseshoe Bend, AZ


About 400 miles Southeast of Yosemite, a different landscape displays its beauty. In the American Southwest, shades of red and brown eroded sandstone create a stunning desert portrait, full of muted color and rich constrast. While Yosemite is my favorite single location, the desert is by far my favorite geographical region. I have traveled to many desert locations, but I must warn you, the summer heat is no joke.



The Pacific Northwest is known for its rain, and while it can sometimes become mundane, the copious amount of water produces some of the most spectacular waterfalls in the world!

Photo Credit: Robert Holloway ® 2018. Location: Toketee Falls, OR


Yes, there is plenty of rain in the rainforest, but views like that above make it well worth it, even if it means dealing with wet shoes and socks all day.

Below is a list of my top three National Parks, if I absolutely had to rank them.
(Note:This is EXTREMELY biased)


  1. Yosemite National Park, CA

    2. What more can I say? Yosemite looks likes its out of a fantasy world!

  2. Zion National Park, UT

    3. Zion is a hiker's paradise. Check out these iconic hikes if you're in the park!
    • Angel's Landing
    • Observation Point
    • West Rim Trail


  3. Joshua Tree National Park,CA

    4. Talk about otherworldly. The Joshua Trees are a must-see if you're in Southern California.
